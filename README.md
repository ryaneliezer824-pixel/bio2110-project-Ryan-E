# Analyzing Drug–Gene Interactions in Cancer Therapy and Drug Metabolism

## Team Members
- Ryan Eliezer

## Project Description
This project analyzes how cancer drugs interact with human genes and how those drugs are processed in the body. The study focuses on identifying the genes most commonly targeted by FDA-approved cancer drugs and determining which drugs interact with CYP450 enzymes involved in drug metabolism. Understanding these interactions helps explain treatment effectiveness, drug metabolism, and differences in patient response.

## Research Question
- Which human genes are most commonly targeted by FDA-approved cancer drugs?
- Which cancer drugs interact with CYP450 enzymes involved in drug metabolism?

## Dataset
- Drug-Gene Interaction Database (DGIdb)
